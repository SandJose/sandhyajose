Sandhya Jose

I am a Project Scientist at the Climate Research and Services wing of Indian Meteorological Department,India, focusing on atmospheric research and climate studies. I completed my doctoral studies from CSIR-National Physical Laboratory, under the mentorship of Dr. Sachchidanand Singh. Over the years, I have had the opportunities to be the part of various research projects at Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Indian Institute of Tropical Meteorology (IITM) and Physical Research Laboratory (PRL).

My research focuses on aerosol cloud radiation interaction, emphasising on the South Asian monsoonal and pre-monsoonal cloud systems. I am particularly interested in how aerosol particle size distribution influences cloud dynamics. In the future, I am to expand the scope of my thesis work incorporating both the physical and chemical aspects of aerosols in altering the cloud dynamics. I am also interested in the broader issues of new particle formations (NPF events), their reaction pathways and Cloud Condensation Nuclei (CCN) activation efficiency. As a climate change enthusiast, I am strongly motivated to work on climate change mitigation and awareness alongside my core research areas.

Research Interests

  • Cloud Physics : Aerosol-Cloud Interactions, Cloud micro-physical processes, Cloud Radiative Forcing
  • Aerosols : Aerosol optical properties and Radiative Forcing, Aerosol Physical and Chemical Characteristics , Aerosol Particle Size Distribution and new particle formation events.
  • Climate Change : Energy and Climate Policy, Climate Politics, Sustainable Development, Environmental Assessment Impact
  • Environmental Data Analysis

I am currently exploring the impacts of climate change on monsoon patterns in India, with a focus on understanding how these changes influence weather forecasting accuracy.
